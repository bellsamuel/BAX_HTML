Scott Baxendale

Scott is our driving force of nature! Cultivating a career of building, repairing, and restoring guitars over more than 40 years, what Scott doesn’t know, might not be worth knowing. Originating from Winfield, Kansas, starting at Mossman Guitars, Scott has carved out a unique place in the guitar world, working with the likes of Billy Gibbons, Johnny Cash, Hank Williams, Jr., Steve Howe, Elvis Costello, Carl Perkins, Joe Walsh, Willie Nelson, Jeff Tweedy, Jason Isbell, Booker T Jones, and Drive-By-Truckers.

If the workbench load can be cleared, Scott’s limited free time is spent collecting vinyl and making records in his private recording studio. His full and detailed biography can be found here: http://baxendaleguitar.com/custom_bio.html

Pamela Baxendale

Pam is our managerial leader, manning the phones, keeping the team in check, and logistically streamlining our workflow. The friendly face at the front desk, steering the ship from the helm and guiding our chaos into order. Pam’s guitar knowledge and superior client-facing skills ensure your vintage guitar finds its way to glorious reinvention.


When not in the shop Pam has a perceptive eye for an image, with personal projects as a photographer and artist. Making faces from used guitar parts and auctioning for charities such as Nuci’s Space, and line art keeps Pam delving through used parts and wood store and exploring for external inspiration.

Joel Byron

Joel is a permanent fixture in our shop. His expertise as a prominent luthier, tech, builder and musician, is invaluable both in carrying out rebuilds, expert repairs, precision setups and teaching our students. Joel’s skills are in constant demand, and we often can’t get enough of him with his other full-time gig as personal guitar tech to Jimmy Herring and Widespread Panic.


When not filling his time with working on guitars, Joel can be found trying to wind the perfect pick-ups and playing virtuoso fingerpicking guitar. He’s our go-to player for demonstrations and is often found throughout our social media channels.

Timi Conley

Timi is a multi-talented facet of our well-oiled machine, from a temporary entity, to a fully paid-up member. Timi is a proficient guitar builder and repairman, residing with us as a logistical supervisor, interfacing the front desk, shop, marketing, and organizational capacities. Timi’s marketing prowess and on-the-tools skills are immeasurable to our success and proficient turn around.


Timi is a creative force, playing guitar and writing in countless bands, including Wonderland Rangers, Kite to the Moon, and Aqualove while organizing the unstoppable extravaganza that is the Wild Rumpus Parade and Spectacle that takes place on Halloween, downtown Athens, GA.

The Shop

Our shop is a buzzing hive of activity. We straddle two floors of industrial space within the legendary music row district of Athens GA.

Sharing the neighborhood with Chase Park Transduction recording studios and Greer Amplification Company, we are at the heart of the local music scene.

We are equipped with two full machine shops, showroom, wood storage, bench areas and spray booth. Meaning our constant flow of customer guitars are always in differing stages of production.

In addition, we have collectors-items rack of guitars waiting to enter manufacture, these guitars are high-end, rare, models that we collect as we see them for conversation down the line. If you’d like to talk to us about our stock, or have your own guitar you’d like to end us: drop us a line.
